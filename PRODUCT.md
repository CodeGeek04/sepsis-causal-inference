# Product Context

## Name

Causal Notes, an interactive explainer for the paper "LLM-Extracted Covariates for Clinical Causal Inference: Rethinking Integration Strategies".

## Users

- Researchers reading the paper for the first time
- Students who understand math but want causal-inference ideas explained plainly
- Clinicians or ML practitioners who need intuition before code

## Product Purpose

Turn a dense clinical causal-inference paper into a step-by-step, visual, interactive learning experience. The page should make the paper feel teachable rather than intimidating. Every section should show what exists at that stage of the pipeline, what gets added next, and why it matters.

## Tone

- Serious but not sterile
- Clear, calm, and explanatory
- Intellectually ambitious without sounding academic for its own sake
- Visually rich, but never gimmicky

## Strategic Principles

- Build understanding in layers: each step starts from what we already have.
- Prefer concrete examples over abstract definitions.
- Make hidden ideas visible with diagrams, timelines, and controlled interactions.
- Keep the science honest: demo examples illustrate intuition, paper numbers support claims.
- Let the user feel progress through the pipeline instead of reading a wall of text.

## Anti-References

- Generic startup landing pages
- Purple-on-black AI aesthetic
- Endless card grids
- Flat textbook pages with no motion or interaction
